# Tracker signup study guide

The staged plan: **Cinemageddon → MoreThanTV → PassThePopcorn → Karagarga**.
Each one earns access (or builds résumé) for the next. Edit this file
(`dashboard/public/study-guide.md`) freely — it's just markdown rendered into
the panel.

---

## Stage 1 — Cinemageddon (CG)

Cult, horror, exploitation, grindhouse. Easiest of the four. Open signups
appear a few times a year on /r/OpenSignups.

**When signup is open** — usually just registration. Sometimes a short
interview by a staff member.

**If interviewed, expect questions like:**
- Favourite cult / horror / exploitation directors
- Where do you currently get your films from?
- What can you contribute to the tracker (uploads, seeding, requests)?

**Minimum prep:**
- Know directors: Jess Franco, Lucio Fulci, Dario Argento, Jodorowsky,
  Ken Russell, Russ Meyer, John Waters, Herschell Gordon Lewis, Mario Bava
- Know waves: giallo, ozploitation, cannibal/zombi cycle, mondo, J-horror,
  pinky violence, women-in-prison, video nasties
- Have a Letterboxd that demonstrates genuine interest (not just the AFI 100)
- Have a ratio plan: seedbox or always-on home seedbox? (CG cares about
  ratio more than the artier trackers do)

**Goal:** join, seed widely, build ratio + history. CG ratio is later useful
as résumé evidence for MTV / PTP applications.

## Stage 2 — MoreThanTV (MTV)

TV-focused: BBC, broadcast, documentaries. Open signups rare. Application
process when invitations are out.

**Expect questions about:**
- Where do you currently get TV from?
- Favourite shows + sources
- Why this tracker specifically (do not say "for ratio")

**Prep:**
- Have a Trakt or similar that shows real watch history
- Know the difference between WEB-DL / WEBRip / HDTV / Bluray sources
- Know season pack vs episode-by-episode tradeoffs (groups: NTb, FLUX, KOGi,
  CAKES, EDITH for current; for older shows look at internal tags)

## Stage 3 — PassThePopcorn (PTP)

Cinephile movie tracker. Rigorous interview when open. The interview is
the entire bar — they want demonstrated film knowledge, not just a
willingness to seed.

**The interview is a real interview.** Live IRC chat with a staff member,
typically 30–60 minutes. They ask open-ended questions about cinema and
follow up on your answers. Faking it does not work.

**Topics they probe:**
- Film history: silent era, classical Hollywood, French New Wave, New
  Hollywood, Dogme 95
- Auteur theory + your favourite directors and *why*
- Specific films: be ready to discuss style, themes, cinematography
- Format/source knowledge: REMUX vs encode, x264 vs x265, what Bluray
  encoders you respect (CtrlHD, EbP, DON, FraMeSToR)
- Existing trackers / ratio history (CG ratio helps here)

**Prep that actually works:**
- AFI 100 + BFI Sight & Sound poll (both directors' and critics')
- Pick 3–5 directors you genuinely love — be able to name 4–5 films each
  and articulate *why* you love them
- Watch one film a week from a country you don't usually watch (Iranian,
  South Korean, Senegalese, Romanian New Wave)
- Know Criterion well — which directors they champion, recent releases
- Be honest about your gaps. They prefer "I haven't seen much silent
  cinema yet, but I've been working through Murnau" over confident BS.

**What kills applications:**
- Naming only Hollywood blockbusters
- Saying you want PTP "because the quality is better"
- Bullshitting about a film you haven't actually seen
- Asking about ratio / freeleech in the interview

## Stage 4 — Karagarga (KG)

World cinema, arthouse, non-English, rare/out-of-print. The hardest of the
four. Open signups happen maybe once a year, sometimes less.

**The interview is harder than PTP's** — they assume you've already passed
PTP-tier scrutiny and probe deeper into world cinema, niche national
cinemas, and the rarer end of arthouse.

**Topics they probe:**
- National cinemas: Iranian (Kiarostami, Farhadi, Panahi), Turkish (Ceylan),
  Romanian New Wave (Mungiu, Puiu, Porumboiu), Hungarian (Béla Tarr, Jancsó),
  Senegalese (Sembène, Mambéty), Thai (Apichatpong), Filipino (Diaz, Brocka)
- Slow cinema: Tarr, Tarkovsky, Tsai Ming-liang, Lav Diaz, Jeanne Dielman
- Avant-garde: Brakhage, Mekas, Snow, Akerman
- Specific essential figures: Pasolini, Bresson, Ozu, Mizoguchi, Bergman,
  Dreyer, Kiarostami, Tarkovsky, Tarr, Akerman
- Film festivals you follow (Cannes, Berlinale, Locarno, Rotterdam)
- Where you currently fill in rare/OOP films from (Karagarga is *the*
  source for things that aren't on any other tracker)

**Prep that actually works:**
- A multi-year Letterboxd with serious world-cinema viewing history
- Know the Sight & Sound 250 thoroughly
- Read Mubi Notebook, Senses of Cinema, Film Comment
- Watch through the Criterion + Mubi catalogues' arthouse end
- Be able to name *contemporary* arthouse directors, not just dead canonical ones
- Have an answer for "what are 5 films you'd seed that no one else would have?"

**Best signal you can give:** demonstrate you'll *upload rare stuff*, not
just download. They care about preservation. If you have OOP DVDs / VHS rips
you could seed, mention it.
